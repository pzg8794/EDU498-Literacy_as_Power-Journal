***Exploring Literacy as Power: Bridging Social Justice and Critical Communication***

*10 possible  points (Last Revised Jan. 2026\)*

In this assignment, you'll dive into the intersection of literacy and social justice, exploring how literacy can either empower or exclude communities. This project 

gives you a chance to critically reflect on the role of literacy in social change

**Objective:**  
To engage in critical analysis and examine the role literacy plays in communicating social justice issues, with an emphasis on how literacy can either empower or exclude communities. 

**Instructions: Organize the following components in a format of your choosing. ( ex: presentation/paper/ podcast, etc)** 

**Research & Documentation:**

* Identify and define a historically relevant social justice topic in your field of study or local community. Why is this issue important to draw attention to?

* Collect relevant sources, such as articles, reports, community narratives, and online discussions about the issue. Cite these sources\!

* Pay attention to the language, tone, and terminology used in the sources.

**Conduct a Critical Analysis:**

* Investigate and detail  how literacy influences the way this issue is communicated to the public. 

* Examine whether specific jargon or terminology might exclude certain groups from understanding or engaging with the issue.

* Consider how the literacy levels of different audiences impact the effectiveness of communication.

**Connect Issue to the Present**: Connect to a  social media **campaign, article, or initiative** related to your selected issue and analyze the following. **Include images/ screenshots:**

* The target audience and their expected literacy levels.

* The strategies used to make the information accessible and understandable.

* Potential communication barriers caused by literacy challenges.

* All of this is included in the reflection

**Recommendations:**  
Propose strategies to address an existing gap in communication about the issue you chose. Ensure these strategies are inclusive and accessible to a broad audience, regardless of literacy levels. Consider using multimedia, simplifying language, or facilitating community-based dialogue.

**Critical Reflection:**  
Reflect on your findings ( this is a synthesis of the issue, the research and documentation, the critical analysis, the connecting artifact such as a social media campaign, an article or an initiative as well as your recommendations). Reflect on the importance of literacy in mediating social justice issues, drawing on personal experiences or observations that have shaped your understanding.
