# Assignment Requirements and Rubric

## Assignment

**Exploring Literacy as Power: Bridging Social Justice and Critical Communication**

**Point value:** 10 possible points, according to the assignment sheet.

## Objective

Engage in critical analysis and examine the role literacy plays in communicating social justice issues, with emphasis on how literacy can either empower or exclude communities.

## Required Components

### 1. Research and Documentation

- Identify and define a historically relevant social justice topic in the student's field of study or local community.
- Explain why the issue is important to draw attention to.
- Collect relevant sources, such as articles, reports, community narratives, and online discussions.
- Cite sources appropriately.
- Pay attention to language, tone, and terminology used in the sources.

### 2. Critical Analysis

- Investigate how literacy influences the way the issue is communicated to the public.
- Examine whether jargon or terminology might exclude groups from understanding or engaging with the issue.
- Consider how the literacy levels of different audiences affect communication.

### 3. Connection to the Present

Connect the issue to a present-day social media campaign, article, or initiative. Analyze:

- Target audience and expected literacy levels.
- Strategies used to make the information accessible and understandable.
- Potential communication barriers caused by literacy challenges.
- Include images or screenshots.

### 4. Recommendations

Propose inclusive and accessible strategies to address a communication gap connected to the selected issue. Strategies may include multimedia, simplified language, or community-based dialogue.

### 5. Critical Reflection

Reflect on the findings by synthesizing:

- The issue.
- The research and documentation.
- The critical analysis.
- The present-day artifact or initiative.
- The recommendations.
- Personal experiences or observations that shaped understanding of literacy and social justice.

## Rubric Summary

| Category | Points | Full-credit expectation |

| Connect Issue to Present | 3 | Present-day initiative is analyzed with target audience, literacy levels, accessibility strategies, communication barriers, and images/screenshots. |
| Recommendations | 2 | Strategies are practical, inclusive, accessible, and connected to the analysis. |
| Reflection | 2 | Reflection shows insight, personal connection, growth, and synthesis. |

## Working Alignment Notes for This Repository

The current project is organized around communication-access injustice in STEM education. It uses a BIO/STEM communication-access case as the evidence site, connects the issue to UDL/CAST Guidelines 3.0 as the present-day initiative, includes original visuals in the body, proposes communication-access design recommendations, and ends with a personal critical reflection connected to teaching practice and equity-oriented systems work.
